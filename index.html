All about Guinea Pigs

Guinea pigs are a domesticated species of rodent. They were originally native to South America.
They have been popular for thousands of years as pets and as food.
Despite their common name, guinea pigs are not native to Guinea, nor are biologically related to pigs, and the origin of the name is still unclear.
Lifespam: 4-8 years.

Guinea Pig's Diet.

  1. Clean water
  2. Grass hay
  3. Fresh vegetables
  4. Pellets

Guinea Pig Breeds

Peruvian Guinea Pig
This breed needs to be groomed almost daily as their fur is so long. It can get matted and dirty easily.

Skinny Guidea Pig
The Skinny Pig is an almost hairless breed of Guinea pig. Skinny pig typically have hair on their muzzles,feet, and legs, but are hairless over the remainder of their bodies.

Abyssinian Guinea pig
The Abyssinian guinea pig is set apart from other breeds by its coat, which is marked with radially growing swirls or cowlicks of hair referred to as rosettes.

Teddy Guinea Pig
Teddy Guinea Pig are cuddly animals with a short coat that grows outward. Their fur can be either soft or tough like a brillo pad. Because of how Their fur grows, they look like teddy bears.
